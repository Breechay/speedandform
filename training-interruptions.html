Stop If the problem persists even at easy effort, end the run and resume the schedule the next day.

              Running interruptions are signals, not verdicts. Adjust the load and let rhythm return.
              When a Session Stops Early

              Sometimes a session begins normally and then stops early.

              The rhythm is off. The body is present but the system is not fully online. This is often the result of accumulated fatigue, disrupted sleep, or schedule friction.

              Stopping the session is often the correct decision.

              Do not attempt to repeat the workout later that day or the next day. Threshold work relies on repeatable signals. When the system is not ready, forcing the session produces distorted effort and poor mechanics.

              Instead:

              • Continue with the week as scheduled
              • Let the next threshold session arrive normally
              • Focus on sleep and rhythm for the following days

              Missing one session has almost no impact on a training cycle.

              Forcing the wrong one can disrupt several.

              If form begins to drift during the run, first reduce intensity and allow the rhythm to settle. If the pattern disappears at easy effort, the run can usually continue as an easy session.

              If the pattern persists even at easy pace, end the run and reset for the next day.

              Repeated occurrences across multiple runs should be treated as an interruption pattern rather than a single-session anomaly.
