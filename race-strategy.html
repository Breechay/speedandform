How FORM athletes approach competition. First Principle
Race pace is not chosen — it is revealed

You don't decide your race pace on race day. You build it in training, protect it in taper, and allow it to emerge when the conditions are right. The athlete's job on race day is not to interrupt the process.

Threshold ≠ race pace

Threshold sessions are done pre-fatigued, without race support — no taper freshness, no adrenaline, no continuous flow. When a workout feels hard at a given pace, that is normal training stress, not a prediction of what you can hold for 13 miles. The two are not the same number.

The ceiling is as important as the floor

Every phase has a floor (target) and a ceiling (hard limit). The ceiling is what you do not exceed, especially in the opening miles. Adrenaline at the start line is the most expensive mistake in distance running. If your watch reads faster than your ceiling in mile 2, back off immediately — no exceptions.

The Three-Phase Structure
Opening
Settle. Don't move.

8–12 sec/mi slower than race pace. This feels too easy. That is correct. The opening phase lets the body find its rhythm without interference from the crowd, the excitement, or the ego.

If it feels comfortable, you're doing it right. If it feels slow, you're probably still doing it right.

Middle
Locked. No decisions.

3–7 sec/mi slower than race pace. Most races are won or lost here — not by heroics at the end, but by quietly drifting 5 seconds per mile too fast through the middle. No forcing. No reacting to runners around you. Stay inside the rhythm.

One decision in the middle: none.

Last 5K
Allow compression.

Race pace ± 5 sec/mi. The final phase is not a push — it is the natural consequence of having run the first two phases correctly. If you executed opening and middle well, the last 5K tightens on its own. If you have to force it, the middle went wrong.

Don't push. Stay with it. Let it come.

Watch Behavior
LOOK Current pace in miles 1–3. If it's faster than your opening ceiling — back off immediately. Adrenaline is lying to you.
LOOK Average pace at the halfway point. If it's faster than your middle ceiling, you've already borrowed from the back half. Settle now, not later.
IGNORE Individual mile splits. One slow mile is nothing. One fast mile early is the race-killer.
IGNORE The runners around you. Their race is not your race. Their pace is not your pace.
IGNORE How you feel in the middle miles. It will feel moderate. That's correct. Don't interpret "this feels okay" as permission to push.
Fueling · The Rule
Schedule, not hunger

At half marathon pace, you burn through glycogen in roughly 75–90 minutes. You will hit the wall before you feel depleted — by the time you feel it, it's too late. Fuel on a schedule. This is the most common first-race mistake at this level.

Night before
Carbohydrate load. Simple, familiar foods. Pasta, rice, bread, potatoes. No heavy sauces, no new foods, nothing high-fiber. 600–800 calories of carbs. Drink water consistently through the evening.
Race morning
400–600 cal, 2.5–3 hrs before gun. Nothing new. Whatever you've eaten before a long run. Oats, toast, banana, white rice. Avoid high-fat foods. Coffee is fine if it's your routine.
30 min out
Gel #1 — the pre-load. 25–30 minutes before the gun. Tops off blood glucose. Chase with water. Timing matters — don't do this at the start line.
Mile 4–5
Gel #2 — first in-race. Don't wait to feel depleted. Take with water at the next aid station.
Mile 8–9
Gel #3 — the critical one. The most important gel. Miles 9–11 are where glycogen hits. This puts fuel in your bloodstream exactly when you need it. Don't skip it.
Every aid
4–6 oz water at every station. Thirst lags actual need by 10–15 minutes. Don't skip stations even if you feel fine.
The Three Days Before
Friday
Complete rest.

No running. Lay out your gear tonight — every item. Carb dinner. Sleep early. The morning should require zero decisions.

Saturday
10–15 min shakeout + 4 strides.

Strides at race pace, full walk recovery between. This is the last time you run fast before the race. It should feel effortless — taper is working. No alcohol tonight.

Sunday
Run the first three miles only.

Wake early, eat immediately, leave unhurried. Warmup 40 minutes before gun: 10 min easy jog, dynamic drills, 4 strides at goal pace. Get away from people who are nervous — their energy is contagious. Take one breath at the start line. You know what the first three miles look like. Run those. The rest follows.

Race Day
The only three instructions
Opening Almost boring. If it feels easy, you're right.
Middle Locked. No decisions. Stay inside the rhythm.
Last 5K No push. Stay with it. Let it come to you.
Finish neutral. Not heroic. Not collapsing. Neutral.
That is how you maximize what the training built.
